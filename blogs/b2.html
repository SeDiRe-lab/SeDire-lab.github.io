Teritarial Landslide Early Warning System

Landslinde is a natural disaster that occurs in many parts of the world. It is a major threat to human life and infrastructure. The Himalayan region is one of the most landslide-prone regions in the world. The Himalayan region covers about 18% of the total land area of India and is inhabited by about 50 million people. The Himalayan region is characterized by high seismicity, high rainfall, and steep slopes. The Himalayan region is also characterized by deforestation, mining, and construction activities. All these factors make the Himalayan region highly susceptible to landslides.

So far, the Himalayan region has witnessed many landslides that have caused huge loss of life and property. The Kedarnath landslide of 2013 is one of the most devastating landslides in the Himalayan region. The Kedarnath landslide occurred on 16th June 2013 in the Mandakini valley of Uttarakhand. The Kedarnath landslide was triggered by heavy rainfall. The Kedarnath landslide caused huge loss of life and property. The Kedarnath landslide was a major disaster that occurred in the Himalayan region.
